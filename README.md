Design Critique: PromptCue
What is working well:
1. High-Impact Copy: The hero headline is punchy and addresses the "ROI" gap in enterprise AI.
2. Visual Hierarchy: The use of cards for tool selection (Power BI, M365, etc.) is very clear. The "Most Popular" and "Largest Install Base" tags are great social proof.
3. Modern UI: The "Live Prompt Generator" window with the code-snippet look feels technical and authoritative—exactly what an analyst or developer wants to see.
Areas for Enterprise Improvement:
1. The "Trust Gap" (Social Proof)
Enterprise buyers rarely buy from a site without a "Logo Cloud."
• Recommendation: Below your hero section (and before the "4 steps" section), add a monochrome row of logos of companies that could use this. Even if you don't have clients yet, use "Trusted by professionals at..."
2. The Navigation Bar
• Issue: The "Pricing" and "Account" links are a bit small and lack a primary "Call to Action" (CTA).
• Recommendation: Make "Open Studio" or "Book a Demo" a prominent button in the top right of the Nav Bar so it's always visible.
3. Visual Depth in the Cards
• Issue: The white cards on the light-grey background (in the "Studio" selection) are a bit flat.
• Recommendation: Add a subtle 1px border or a soft "glow" to the active/hovered card to make it feel more interactive.
4. The "Steps" Section
• Issue: Enterprise users like to see the process.
• Recommendation: Ensure your "Four steps to a prompt" section uses high-quality icons or small looping UI animations (Lottie files) to show the value quickly.
5. Accessibility & Readability
• Check: Ensure the blue text on the dark background in your hero section meets WCAG contrast ratios. It looks a bit dark on some mobile screens.
